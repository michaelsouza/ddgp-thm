## 1. Overview and Contribution

This article presents a mathematically elegant, computationally efficient **rank-count theory** for computing the size of local realization sets in the **Discretizable Distance Geometry Problem (DDGP)**. While the Molecular DDGP (DMDGP) has a well-developed symmetry theory based on consecutive predecessor interval reflections, general DDGP instances feature reflections that propagate dynamically through non-consecutive dependency structures. 

The paper's core contribution is an $\mathbb{F}_2$ rank-nullity formulation that avoids exponential Branch-and-Prune (BP) branch-code enumeration. By constructing graph-combinatorial cone and base generators (the mask matrix $M$) and representing active-edge incompatibilities as mirror-labeled rows in a violation matrix $V$, the paper proves that the local feasible branch codes form an affine coset of $M(\ker V)$.

While the underlying theory is solid and verified by comprehensive stress testing (matching exact counts across thousands of instances), the manuscript in its current form has several structural, typographical, and presentation flaws that must be resolved before it can be considered a **camera-ready paper** in a high-impact applied mathematics journal.

## 2. Structural & LaTeX Formatting Flaws

### 2.1 Lack of Table Float Environments and Improper Grid Rules
In **Section 8 (Worked Example)**, the generator support data (lines 466–475) and active-edge violation data (lines 501–510) are typeset inside unnumbered raw math displays using `\begin{array}{c|c|c}`:
```latex
\[
\begin{array}{c|c|c}
\text{generator} & \text{support} & \text{mirror clique}\\
\hline
g_4 & \{v_4,v_6\} & C_1\\
...
\end{array}
\]
```
* **Flaw:** These are key data structures for checking the worked example, but they lack table float encapsulation (`\begin{table}[htbp]`), captions (`\caption`), and cross-referencing labels (`\label`). 
* **Flaw:** Modern camera-ready guidelines for leading mathematics publishers (e.g., SIAM, AMS, Springer) strongly discourage vertical rules and double horizontal lines. The package `booktabs` is imported in the preamble but is completely unused.
* **Correction Required:** Package the arrays as proper numbered tables, remove vertical bars (`|`), and use `\toprule`, `\midrule`, and `\bottomrule`.

### 2.2 Broken Proof Architecture and Missing Q.E.D. Markers
* **Flaw:** The proof of the main result, **Theorem 1 (Feasible branch shift theorem)**, is separated into a custom section (`\section{Proof of the Feasible Branch Shift Theorem}`) rather than using a standard nested LaTeX `\begin{proof} ... \end{proof}` block.
* **Flaw:** Because it is formatted as raw text under a custom header, it lacks the automated mathematical Q.E.D. box (`\qed` or `\qedsymbol`). This violates basic AMS mathematical typesetting standards.
* **Correction Required:** The proof should be inline immediately following the theorem statement or its corollary, using `\begin{proof}` and `\end{proof}` blocks.

### 2.3 Missing Metadata (Keywords & Mathematical Subject Classification)
* **Flaw:** Applied mathematics papers require standard indexing metadata. The current draft completely lacks **Keywords** and **MSC2020 classifications**.
* **Correction Required:** Add appropriate classification lines (e.g., Primary `51K05` [Distance geometry]; Secondary `05C62`, `15A03`, `90C27`) directly below the abstract.

## 3. Mathematical Style & Precision Flaws

### 3.1 Unbraced Subscripts in Standard Sets
* **Flaw:** The manuscript repeatedly uses `\mathbb F_2` and `\mathbb R^K` instead of `\mathbb{F}_2` and `\mathbb{R}^K`.
* **Flaw:** While some LaTeX parsers compile `\mathbb F_2` successfully, it is structurally flawed because the subscript is applied to the character `F` without grouping. Standard mathematical LaTeX requires grouping: `\mathbb{F}_2` and `\mathbb{R}^K`.

### 3.2 Overly Brief Geometry in Lemma 2 (Zero-Violation Preservation)
* **Flaw:** The proof of Lemma 2 states: *"Applying these block reflections clique by clique preserves every edge in $F$, and their net branch mask is $\bigoplus_C S_C = M\alpha = h$."*
* **Flaw:** Reflections in Euclidean space generally do not commute, and reflecting a block can dynamically move the hyperplanes representing subsequent mirror cliques. The proof does not explain why the order of composition is well-defined or why the subsequent mirrors are preserved under previous block reflections.
* **Correction Required:** Clarify that since active edges crossing $S_C$ have their fixed endpoints lying directly *on* the mirror hyperplane $\operatorname{aff}(C)$, the reflection is symmetric, and subsequent mirror definitions remain invariant.

### 3.3 Vague Exceptional-Set Argument in Theorem 1
* **Flaw:** The proof of Theorem 1 states: *"If $h \notin \mathcal{K}_F$, then by generic mirror separation this branch shift changes at least one active-edge length outside a proper algebraic exceptional set. This contradicts the fact..."*
* **Flaw:** This is hand-wavy. A camera-ready paper must explicitly define the algebraic parameter space $\mathbb{K}_n$ (the iterated quadratic sign cover of the realization) and state that the actual physical coordinates reside outside a finite union of algebraic hypersurfaces (where coordinate discriminants or specific Cayley-Menger determinants vanish).

## 4. Presentation & Visualization Flaws

* **Flaw:** The paper relies entirely on text to explain graph-combinatorial and geometric dependency structures. For a reader trying to follow the worked example in Section 8, visualizing the directed graph, predecessor sets, and pruning constraints requires drawing them by hand.
* **Correction Required:** Embed a high-quality **TikZ vector diagram** in Section 8 showing the graph structure of the 6-vertex example, distinguishing between directed predecessor arcs (e.g., $v_1 \to v_4$, $v_4 \to v_6$) and dashed pruning edges (e.g., $\{v_3, v_6\}$).
