## Intuition

A base-clique closure mask is a binary mask supported on all local decision vertices generated by a base clique, together with their dependency descendants.

It is useful because DDGP symmetries need not be generated by single-vertex dependency cones. If several vertices are generated from the same predecessor clique, reflecting them as a block may preserve local base distances even when reflecting only one of them does not.

This captures a symmetry pattern that is visible in the [base-clique transition graph](base-clique-transition-graph.md) but not in the ordinary individual dependency cones.

## Formal definition

Let $G=(V,E,d)$ be a DDGP instance with predecessor sets $U_i$ and dependency DAG $D_U$. For a pruning edge $e=\{u,v\}$, let

$$
L_e=\operatorname{Fix}_U(u)\cup\operatorname{Fix}_U(v)
$$

be the local vertex set, and let

$$
B_e=L_e\setminus V_0
$$

be the local binary decision set after fixing the initial simplex $V_0$.

For a predecessor clique $C$, define the vertices generated from $C$ by

$$
\operatorname{Gen}(C)=\{v_i\in L_e \mid U_i=C\}.
$$

The base-clique closure of $C$ is

$$
\operatorname{Cl}_e(C)
=
B_e
\cap
\bigcup_{w\in \operatorname{Gen}(C)}
\operatorname{Cone}_U(w),
$$

where $\operatorname{Cone}_U(w)$ is the forward dependency cone of $w$.

The corresponding binary mask is the vector

$$
m_C\in\mathbb{F}_2^{B_e},
\qquad
(m_C)_z=1
\Longleftrightarrow
z\in \operatorname{Cl}_e(C).
$$

## Role in local solution counting

Individual endpoint-separating cones can fail to explain the local solution count because some valid symmetries are combinations of several dependency effects.

Base-clique closure masks add a natural family of block toggles. Experiments suggest that the stabilizer of the [local solution code](local-solution-code.md) may be recovered from the span of individual cone masks together with base-clique closure masks.

Whether such a mask preserves a given edge set can be tested using [mirror-compatible graph masks](mirror-compatible-graph-mask.md): crossing edges are allowed only when their fixed endpoint belongs to the mirror clique.
