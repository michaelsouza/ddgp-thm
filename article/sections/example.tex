\section{A Small Worked Example}
\label{sec:example}

We include a fully worked six-vertex example to make the construction checkable by hand. Although the instance is small, it contains both cone and base generators, a nontrivial labeled violation kernel, and a presentation ambiguity that must be removed by the projection $M(\ker V)$.

\subsection{Combinatorial Setup}
\label{sec:example-combo}

Let $K=2$ in $\mathbb{R}^2$. The fixed initial simplex is
\[
V_0=\{v_1, v_2, v_3\}.
\]
Consider the local predecessor sets
\[
U_4=\{v_1, v_3\},
\qquad
U_5=\{v_1, v_3\},
\qquad
U_6=\{v_1, v_4\},
\]
and the pruning edge set
\[
P=\{\{v_3, v_6\},\,\{v_4, v_5\}\}.
\]
The local vertex and decision sets are
\[
L_P=\{v_1, v_3, v_4, v_5, v_6\},
\qquad
B_P=\{v_4, v_5, v_6\}.
\]
There are two predecessor cliques:
\[
C_1=\{v_1, v_3\},
\qquad
C_2=\{v_1, v_4\}.
\]
The first generates $v_4$ and $v_5$; the second generates $v_6$. Since $v_6$ depends on $v_4$, we have
\[
\operatorname{Cone}_U(v_4)\cap B_P=\{v_4, v_6\}.
\]
The graph structure, predecessor dependency arcs, and pruning edges are illustrated in Figure~\ref{fig:worked-example}.

The generator mask matrix is:
\[
M=
\begin{bmatrix}
1&0&0&1&0\\
0&1&0&1&0\\
1&0&1&1&1
\end{bmatrix}.
\]

The active edge set is $F = E_D[L_P] \cup P$, where the local discretization edges are
\[
\{v_1, v_4\},\{v_3, v_4\},
\{v_1, v_5\},\{v_3, v_5\},
\{v_1, v_6\},\{v_4, v_6\}.
\]

Thus, with rows ordered as the two active-edge labels $(\{v_4, v_5\},C_1)$ and $(\{v_3, v_6\},C_2)$, the labeled violation matrix is:
\[
V=
\begin{bmatrix}
1&1&0&0&0\\
0&0&1&0&1
\end{bmatrix}.
\]
Write a generator coefficient vector as $\alpha = (a_4,a_5,a_6,a_{C_1},a_{C_2}) \in \mathbb{F}_2^5$. Then the zero-violation condition $V\alpha = 0$, with arithmetic over $\mathbb{F}_2$, is equivalent to
\[
a_4+a_5=0,
\qquad
a_6+a_{C_2}=0.
\]
Hence, $a_4=a_5=t$, $a_6=a_{C_2}=u$, and $a_{C_1}=w$ is free. Applying the mask matrix gives:
\[
M\alpha
=
t(m_4+m_5)+u(m_6+m_{C_2})+w\,m_{C_1}
=
(t+w)
\begin{bmatrix}
1\\1\\1
\end{bmatrix}.
\]
The parameter $u$ changes the generator presentation but not the branch mask, because $m_6=m_{C_2}$. Therefore,
\[
\mathcal{K}_F
=
M(\ker V)
=
\left\{
\begin{bmatrix}0\\0\\0\end{bmatrix},
\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\1\end{bmatrix}
\right\}.
\]
The ranks are
\[
\operatorname{rank}\begin{bmatrix} M \\ V \end{bmatrix}=3,
\qquad
\operatorname{rank}(V)=2.
\]
Therefore, the local solution count is
\[
|\Xi_F| = 2^{3-2} = 2.
\]
The nontrivial feasible branch shift is the mask $\{v_4, v_5, v_6\}$, obtained from $g_4\oplus g_5$, or equivalently from the base generator $g_{C_1}$. The two feasible local solutions are a reference solution and its simultaneous reflection through the mirror $\operatorname{aff}(v_1, v_3)$ on the local vertices $v_4, v_5, v_6$.

\subsection{Concrete Geometric Realization}
\label{sec:example-geometry}

To ground this algebraic structure, we provide a concrete geometric realization in $\mathbb{R}^2$ with exact coordinates and distances. The coordinates are chosen so that all active distances are positive and can be verified directly.

\paragraph{Reference Coordinate Embedding.}
Place the initial simplex $V_0 = \{v_1, v_2, v_3\}$ at:
\[
v_1 = (0,0), \quad v_2 = (2,0), \quad v_3 = (0,2).
\]
This forms a right isosceles triangle. Now, place the remaining vertices at their reference coordinates:
\[
v_4 = (1, 1), \quad v_5 = (2, 2), \quad v_6 = (2, 0).
\]

The edge lengths for the active set $F = E_D[L_P] \cup P$ are determined by this embedding:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Discretization distances:
    \[
    \begin{aligned}
    d_{14} &= \|(0,0) - (1,1)\|_2 = \sqrt{2}, \quad & d_{34} &= \|(0,2) - (1,1)\|_2 = \sqrt{2}; \\
    d_{15} &= \|(0,0) - (2,2)\|_2 = 2\sqrt{2}, \quad & d_{35} &= \|(0,2) - (2,2)\|_2 = 2; \\
    d_{16} &= \|(0,0) - (2,0)\|_2 = 2, \quad & d_{46} &= \|(1,1) - (2,0)\|_2 = \sqrt{2}.
    \end{aligned}
    \]
    \item Pruning distances:
    \[
    d_{36} = \|(0,2) - (2,0)\|_2 = 2\sqrt{2}, \quad d_{45} = \|(1,1) - (2,2)\|_2 = \sqrt{2}.
    \]
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Reflection Checks.}
Let us analyze what happens under selected generator moves:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Single branch change at $v_5$:}
    The mirror for $v_5$ is $\operatorname{aff}(v_1, v_3)$ (the $y$-axis, $x=0$). Mirroring $v_5 = (2,2)$ across the $y$-axis yields $v_5' = (-2, 2)$. 
    If we change only the branch bit at $v_5$, the pruning edge $\{v_4, v_5\}$ has its distance changed to $\|(1,1) - (-2,2)\|_2 = \sqrt{10} \ne \sqrt{2}$. This represents a single violation of the edge $\{v_4, v_5\}$ by the generator $g_5$.
    
    \item \textbf{Cone move rooted at $v_4$ ($g_4$):}
    The cone generator $g_4$ reflects $v_4$ and its local descendant $v_6$ across the $y$-axis:
    \[
    v_4' = (-1, 1), \quad v_6' = (-2, 0).
    \]
    The discretization distance $\{v_4, v_6\}$ is preserved. The pruning distance $\{v_3, v_6\}$ is also preserved because $v_3$ lies on the mirror. However, the pruning distance to $v_5 = (2,2)$ becomes $\|(-1, 1) - (2, 2)\|_2 = \sqrt{10} \ne \sqrt{2}$. This is the violation of the active edge $\{v_4, v_5\}$ by the generator $g_4$.

    \item \textbf{Alternate branch at $v_6$ ($g_6$):}
    The mirror for $v_6$ is $\operatorname{aff}(v_1, v_4)$, the line $y = x$. Reflecting $v_6=(2,0)$ across this line gives
    \[
    v_6' = (0,2).
    \]
    The predecessor distances to $v_1$ and $v_4$ are preserved, but the pruning distance to $v_3$ changes from $2\sqrt{2}$ to $0$. This is the violation of the active edge $\{v_3, v_6\}$ by the generator $g_6$.
    
    \item \textbf{Simultaneous reflection ($s \oplus \{v_4, v_5, v_6\}$):}
    If we apply the nontrivial feasible branch shift $h = \{v_4, v_5, v_6\}$, we reflect all three vertices across the $y$-axis ($x=0$):
    \[
    v_4' = (-1, 1), \quad v_5' = (-2, 2), \quad v_6' = (-2, 0).
    \]
    The dynamic mirror for $v_6'$ becomes $\operatorname{aff}(v_1, v_4')$, which is $y = -x$. The predecessor distances are preserved by reflection, and the pruning distance to $v_3 = (0,2)$ remains
    \[
    \|(0,2) - (-2,0)\|_2 = 2\sqrt{2} = d_{36}.
    \]
    The pruning distance between $v_4'$ and $v_5'$ is:
    \[
    \|v_4' - v_5'\|_2 = \|(-1, 1) - (-2, 2)\|_2 = \sqrt{2} = d_{45}.
    \]
    All active discretization and pruning distances are preserved, showing that $s^\ast \oplus h$ is the second geometrically valid realization of the local active subproblem.
\end{enumerate}

This coordinate realization verifies the rank count in a case where the generator presentation has a nontrivial kernel and the feasible shift space has dimension one.
