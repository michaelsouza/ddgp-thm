\section{Main Results}
\label{sec:results}

The central objective of this section is to establish a precise equivalence between the physical geometry of distance-preserving branch shifts and the algebraic structure of the binary subspace $\mathcal{K}_F \subseteq \mathbb{F}_2^{B_{\mathrm{c}}}$. Specifically, we show that the algebraic space $\mathcal{K}_F$ is constructively feasible—meaning every shift in $\mathcal{K}_F$ preserves all active edge lengths—and that, under a natural generic separation condition, no other distance-preserving shifts can exist.

Geometrically, moving from a branch code $s$ to $s \oplus h$ via a branch shift $h \in \mathbb{F}_2^{B_{\mathrm{c}}}$ corresponds to a sequence of partial reflections. A shift preserves the validity of all active constraints if it is produced by a generator combination with zero net labeled violations, meaning $\alpha \in \ker V$. Because multiple generator combinations can yield the same branch mask, the candidate feasible branch-shift space is the image of this kernel under the mask matrix $M$:
\[
\mathcal{K}_F = M(\ker V).
\]

\begin{lemma}[Branch-shift rank]
\label{lem:rank}
For the feasible branch shift space $\mathcal{K}_F = M(\ker V)$,
\[
\dim \mathcal{K}_F
=
\operatorname{rank}_{\mathbb{F}_2}\begin{bmatrix} M \\ V \end{bmatrix}
-
\operatorname{rank}_{\mathbb{F}_2}(V).
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let $m = \lvert \mathcal{G} \rvert = \dim A$. Since $\mathcal{K}_F = M(\ker V)$, we restrict $M$ to $\ker V$:
\[
M\big|_{\ker V} : \ker V \to \mathbb{F}_2^{B_{\mathrm{c}}}.
\]
By rank-nullity applied to this restricted map,
\[
\dim \mathcal{K}_F
=
\dim \ker V
-
\dim(\ker V \cap \ker M).
\]
Now $\dim \ker V = m - \operatorname{rank}_{\mathbb{F}_2}(V)$, and
\[
\ker V \cap \ker M
=
\ker\begin{bmatrix} M \\ V \end{bmatrix}.
\]
Therefore,
\[
\dim(\ker V \cap \ker M)
=
m - \operatorname{rank}_{\mathbb{F}_2}\begin{bmatrix} M \\ V \end{bmatrix}.
\]
Substituting these dimensions into the rank-nullity equation gives:
\[
\begin{aligned}
\dim \mathcal{K}_F &= (m - \operatorname{rank}_{\mathbb{F}_2}(V)) - \left(m - \operatorname{rank}_{\mathbb{F}_2}\begin{bmatrix} M \\ V \end{bmatrix}\right) \\
&= \operatorname{rank}_{\mathbb{F}_2}\begin{bmatrix} M \\ V \end{bmatrix} - \operatorname{rank}_{\mathbb{F}_2}(V).
\end{aligned}
\]
This completes the proof.
\end{proof}

The next lemma is the geometric direction encoded by the labeled violation matrix: zero net labeled violation gives a composition of mirror-compatible blocks, hence preserving every active edge.

\begin{lemma}[Zero-violation preservation]
\label{lem:preservation}
Let $\alpha \in \ker V$ and let $h = M\alpha$. If $s \in \Xi_F$, then $s \oplus h \in \Xi_F$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
We group the selected generators in $\alpha$ by mirror clique. For a fixed clique $C$, let $\alpha_C \subseteq \mathcal{G}_C$ be the subset of generators in $\alpha$ with mirror clique $C$, and let $S_C = \bigoplus_{g \in \alpha_C} S_g$ be the XOR sum of their supports. The row $(e,C)$ of $V\alpha$ represents the net violation parity of generators with mirror $C$ against the active edge $e \in F$. Since $V\alpha = 0$, this parity is zero for every active edge $e \in F$ and every mirror clique $C$.

The block $(S_C, C)$ is mirror-compatible with each active edge. Indeed, suppose that an active edge $e = \{a,b\} \in F$ crosses $S_C$, meaning that exactly one endpoint lies in $S_C$. Without loss of generality, assume $a \in S_C$ and $b \notin S_C$. Let $\chi_g: \mathcal{V} \to \{0,1\}$ denote the indicator function of $S_g$ for each $g \in \alpha_C$. The indicator function of $S_C$ satisfies $\chi_{S_C}(v) \equiv \sum_{g \in \alpha_C} \chi_g(v) \pmod 2$. Because generator supports are disjoint from their own mirror cliques, $S_g \cap C = \varnothing$ for all $g \in \alpha_C$, which implies $S_C \cap C = \varnothing$ and hence $a \notin C$. Now, suppose for contradiction that the fixed endpoint $b$ is also not in $C$. A generator $g \in \alpha_C$ violates $e$ with label $C$ if and only if it crosses $e$ (i.e., $|e \cap S_g| = 1$) and the fixed endpoint $e \setminus S_g$ lies outside $C$. Since $a \notin C$ and $b \notin C$, the latter condition is satisfied for every crossing generator. The number of such crossing generators in $\alpha_C$ is 
\[
\sum_{g \in \alpha_C} |e \cap S_g| = \sum_{g \in \alpha_C} (\chi_g(a) + \chi_g(b)) \equiv \chi_{S_C}(a) + \chi_{S_C}(b) \equiv 1 + 0 \equiv 1 \pmod 2.
\]
Thus, an odd number of generators in $\alpha_C$ violate $e$, contributing an odd parity to the row $(e,C)$ of $V\alpha$. This contradicts $V\alpha = 0$. Hence, the fixed endpoint $b$ must lie in $C$, establishing mirror-compatibility.

To show that the combined transformation preserves the lengths of all active edges, we apply the nonzero blocks $(S_C, C)$ sequentially. We order the mirror cliques $C_1, C_2, \dots, C_k$ according to the discretization sequence (so that the corresponding discretization vertices satisfy $v_{C_1} < v_{C_2} < \dots < v_{C_k}$). Because the support of any generator with mirror $C_j$ only contains vertices discretized at or after $v_{C_j}$, we have $S_{C_j} \subseteq \{v_{C_j}, \dots, n\}$. Since $C_i \subseteq \{1, \dots, v_{C_i}-1\}$ and $v_{C_i} < v_{C_j}$ for all $j > i$, it follows that $S_{C_j} \cap C_i = \varnothing$. 

We apply the blocks in the reverse discretization order: $C_k, C_{k-1}, \dots, C_1$, and argue by induction. At the step for $C_i$, the only blocks applied so far are those for $C_j$ with $j > i$. Since $S_{C_j} \cap C_i = \varnothing$ for all $j > i$, the vertices of $C_i$ have not been toggled by any previous steps, so they lie exactly on their original coordinates, spanning the original mirror hyperplane $\operatorname{aff}(C_i)$. For any edge $e = \{u,v\} \in F$:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item If both endpoints are reflected or neither endpoint is reflected by $S_{C_i}$, the current length of $e$ is preserved by the rigid isometry of the reflection.
    \item If exactly one endpoint is reflected (say $u \in S_{C_i}$ and $v \notin S_{C_i}$), mirror-compatibility guarantees that the fixed endpoint $v$ lies in $C_i$. Since $v \in C_i$, its position has not been toggled at this or any previous step, so it lies on $\operatorname{aff}(C_i)$. Therefore, reflecting $u$ across $\operatorname{aff}(C_i)$ preserves the distance between $u$ and $v$.
\end{enumerate}
Thus, each block preserves all active-edge lengths at the moment it is applied. After all blocks are applied, the accumulated branch mask is $\bigoplus_C S_C = M\alpha = h$, and therefore $s \oplus h \in \Xi_F$.
\end{proof}

Lemma~\ref{lem:preservation} proves the constructive inclusion: shifts in $\mathcal{K}_F$ generate feasible constrained codes from any feasible reference code. The converse could fail only if a shift outside $\mathcal{K}_F$ preserved all active distances by an accidental geometric coincidence. The following hypothesis excludes exactly those extra solutions.

\begin{definition}[Mirror-separated parameters]
\label{def:mirror-sep}
Fix the ordered DDGP template, including the predecessor sets and the active edge set $F$. Thus $M$, $V$, and $\mathcal{K}_F=M(\ker V)$ are fixed. Let $\Theta$ be the nondegenerate parameter domain over which the lateration embeddings $x^\theta(s)$ are well-defined for all branch codes $s$.

For $\theta\in\Theta$, write $x^\theta(s)$ for the embedding associated with a branch code $s$. We say that $\theta$ is mirror-separated if, for every shift $h \notin \mathcal{K}_F$ and every branch code $s$ for which both embeddings $x^\theta(s)$ and $x^\theta(s\oplus h)$ are defined, there exists an active edge $\{a,b\} \in F$ such that
\[
\|x^\theta_a(s \oplus h) - x^\theta_b(s \oplus h)\|_2^2 \neq \|x^\theta_a(s) - x^\theta_b(s)\|_2^2.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Genericity and probability one]
Mirror separation rules out accidental geometric coincidences in which a shift $h\notin\mathcal{K}_F$ nevertheless preserves all active edge distances. For a fixed ordered template, failure of mirror separation is described by algebraic equalities in the parameters. When these equalities define a proper exceptional set in the relevant nondegenerate parameter component, that set has relative measure zero. Consequently, any probability distribution on $\Theta$ that is absolutely continuous with respect to the corresponding relative Lebesgue measure assigns probability zero to this failure set. In this conditional sense, mirror separation holds with probability one for continuous nondegenerate parameter choices.
\end{remark}

The main theorem states that, under mirror separation, the converse also holds on the pruning-constrained branch set. The full count then follows by multiplying by the free branch decisions in $B_{\mathrm{f}}$.

\begin{theorem}[Rank-count theorem]
\label{thm:main}
Assume $K \ge 2$ and $\Xi \ne \varnothing$. Assume also that the present predecessor and edge-length parameters are mirror-separated in the sense of Definition~\ref{def:mirror-sep}. Then, for any reference solution $s^\ast \in \Xi$, with constrained restriction $s^\ast_{\mathrm{c}}=s^\ast|_{B_{\mathrm{c}}}$,
\[
\Xi_F
=
s^\ast_{\mathrm{c}} + \mathcal{K}_F
=
s^\ast_{\mathrm{c}} + M(\ker V).
\]
Consequently,
\[
\lvert \Xi\rvert
=
2^{
f+
\operatorname{rank}_{\mathbb{F}_2}\begin{bmatrix} M \\ V \end{bmatrix}
-
\operatorname{rank}_{\mathbb{F}_2}(V)
}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Fix a reference solution $s^\ast \in \Xi$, and let $s^\ast_{\mathrm{c}}=s^\ast|_{B_{\mathrm{c}}}$. We prove that the feasible constrained branch differences from $s^\ast_{\mathrm{c}}$ are exactly $\mathcal{K}_F$.

First, let $h \in \mathcal{K}_F$. By definition, $h = M\alpha$ for some $\alpha \in \ker V$. By Lemma~\ref{lem:preservation} (Zero-violation preservation),
\[
s^\ast_{\mathrm{c}} \oplus h \in \Xi_F.
\]
Hence,
\[
s^\ast_{\mathrm{c}} + \mathcal{K}_F \subseteq \Xi_F.
\]

Conversely, let $s_{\mathrm{c}} \in \Xi_F$ and set $h = s_{\mathrm{c}} \oplus s^\ast_{\mathrm{c}}$. Suppose, for contradiction, that $h \notin \mathcal{K}_F$. Since the present parameters are mirror-separated, the embeddings determined by $s^\ast_{\mathrm{c}}$ and $s_{\mathrm{c}}=s^\ast_{\mathrm{c}}\oplus h$ assign different lengths to at least one active edge in $F$. However, both $s^\ast_{\mathrm{c}}$ and $s_{\mathrm{c}}$ realize every edge in $F$. This contradiction implies that $h$ must belong to $\mathcal{K}_F$, which yields
\[
\Xi_F \subseteq s^\ast_{\mathrm{c}} + \mathcal{K}_F.
\]

Combining both inclusions yields:
\[
\Xi_F = s^\ast_{\mathrm{c}} + \mathcal{K}_F = s^\ast_{\mathrm{c}} + M(\ker V).
\]
By the decomposition in Section~\ref{sec:subproblems}, every feasible constrained code combines with the $2^f$ free branch codes on $B_{\mathrm{f}}$, and no free branch code can affect a pruning edge. Thus
\[
\lvert \Xi\rvert
=
2^f\lvert \Xi_F\rvert
=
2^{f+\dim \mathcal{K}_F}.
\]
Applying Lemma~\ref{lem:rank} gives the displayed rank formula.
\end{proof}
